The Other You

You meet someone identical to you — same life, same mind, same start.

But every moral decision you made, they chose the opposite.
They walked away when you stayed.
They betrayed someone you protected.
They kept secrets you exposed.

And they’re thriving. They sleep soundly.
They say: “I did what was right. You just wanted to feel right.”

Suddenly, everything you built your sense of self on — your sacrifices, your principles — feels fragile.

You could open your mind, admit your morals are just one version of the truth. But if you do... what was the point of all your pain?
